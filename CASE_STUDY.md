# InventoryFlow AI – Case Study

---

## Problem

Many small and medium-sized businesses still manage inventory using spreadsheets or disconnected software. This often leads to inaccurate stock counts, duplicate records, delayed purchase decisions, stock shortages, and inefficient warehouse operations. Managing multiple warehouses, suppliers, and customer orders without a centralized system also makes tracking inventory movements and generating business reports difficult.

The goal of this project was to build a centralized, modern, and scalable inventory management platform that simplifies warehouse operations and provides business owners with real-time insights into their inventory.

---

## Approach

To solve this problem, I designed and developed **InventoryFlow AI**, a full-stack MERN application following industry-standard software architecture and best practices.

The application includes secure JWT-based authentication with role-based access control for Admin and Staff users. The backend follows the MVC architecture using Express.js and MongoDB, while the frontend is built with React and Tailwind CSS to provide a responsive and user-friendly interface.

The system includes modules for:

- Authentication & Authorization
- Dashboard & Analytics
- Product Management
- Category Management
- Warehouse Management
- Bin Management
- Supplier Management
- Customer Management
- Purchase Management
- Sales Management
- Inventory Tracking
- Stock Transfers
- Barcode/QR Code Generation
- Reports & Analytics
- CSV Import/Export
- PDF Invoice Generation
- Activity Logs

The application also implements search, filtering, sorting, pagination, form validation, protected routes, reusable components, and proper API error handling to provide a production-ready user experience.

---

The final product is a fully functional Inventory & Warehouse Management System capable of managing inventory across multiple warehouses with real-time stock updates.
